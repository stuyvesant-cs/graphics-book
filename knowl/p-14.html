Instead of testing both possible points, we could test the midpoint, \((x+1, y+\dfrac{1}{2})\text{.}\) The midpoint of the possible pixels is on the border between them, which leaves us with 3 possibilities:
  1. The midpoint is above the line. This means the line is mostly below the midpoint, and we should choose the lower pixel, \((x+1, y)\text{.}\)
  2. The midpoint to below the line. This means the line is mostly above the midpoint, and we should choose the upper pixel, \((x+1, y+1)\text{.}\)
  3. The midpoint is on the line. In this case, either pixel is a good match.
